**Main Takeaways**

Use **“editorial lifestyle product photography”** as the main direction, not just “cinematic DSLR.”

Your catalogue system should have:

1. **Global style lock**: same lighting, color grade, realism, and mood across every rug.
2. **Shot templates**: same 5 image types for every rug.
3. **Variable interiors**: each rug gets a unique room/background, but the camera logic stays consistent.

**Global Style Lock**

Use this across every prompt:

```text
Premium editorial home decor catalogue photography, realistic DSLR product photography, natural window light, warm afternoon sunlight, soft filmic color grading, gentle highlight rolloff, creamy whites, muted saturation, realistic soft shadows, warm wood tones, balanced warm/cool color separation, interior design magazine aesthetic, tactile textile detail, no HDR, no over-sharpening, no plastic texture, no CGI look.
```

**Shot 1: Wide Room Hero**

Photographer instruction:

```text
Wide straight-on lifestyle room hero shot. Camera at seated eye level, approximately 4-5 feet high, level with straight vertical lines. Rug centered in the foreground and fully visible, styled with furniture around it to show scale. Use a 28-35mm full-frame lens feel. Natural window light from the side or back, bright airy room, soft overexposed windows, warm sunlight on the floor, realistic shadows under furniture.
```

AI prompt add-on:

```text
wide straight-on interior hero shot, rug centered on the floor, full rug visible, furniture arranged around the rug, camera level, straight vertical architecture lines, 35mm DSLR lens, interior design magazine composition
```

**Shot 2: High-Angle Lifestyle Detail**

Photographer instruction:

```text
High-angle lifestyle crop from above, not a flat lay. Camera positioned above the rug and angled down around 50-65 degrees. Show part of a chair, coffee table, or sofa cropped into frame for scale. Rug pattern should dominate the image. Use diagonal composition. Strong natural sunlight patches and shadows across the rug to reveal texture.
```

AI prompt add-on:

```text
high-angle lifestyle detail shot, camera above the rug looking down diagonally, not top-down flat lay, partial furniture visible, rug pattern prominent, sun patches across the rug, realistic interior shadows, tactile textile surface
```

**Shot 3: Studio Corner Detail**

Photographer instruction:

```text
Clean studio product detail of one rug corner on a white or light neutral surface. Camera above the rug at a three-quarter top angle, around 35-50 degrees down. Rug corner placed diagonally in frame with negative space around it. Show edge binding, thickness, weave, and color accuracy. Use soft studio light from upper-left/front.
```

AI prompt add-on:

```text
studio product closeup of rug corner, diagonal composition, white seamless background, three-quarter top-down angle, visible edge binding and rug thickness, soft shadow, accurate textile texture
```

**Shot 4: Texture Macro**

Photographer instruction:

```text
Macro texture detail where the rug fills the entire frame. Camera close to the rug at a shallow oblique angle, around 25-40 degrees above the surface. Focus on pile, weave, fiber texture, and raised pattern. Use side lighting to create small shadows and reveal depth.
```

AI prompt add-on:

```text
macro texture detail of rug surface, frame filled entirely by rug pattern, shallow oblique angle, visible pile height, woven fibers, tactile wool texture, side lighting, crisp but natural focus
```

**Shot 5: Folded Label Detail**

Photographer instruction:

```text
Close product branding detail with one rug corner folded back naturally to reveal the label. Camera at a three-quarter top angle, around 40-55 degrees. Label placed near the upper third of the frame, rug pattern visible underneath and around it. Show flexibility, thickness, edge binding, and textile texture. Soft directional light with gentle fold shadow.
```

AI prompt add-on:

```text
closeup branding detail, one rug corner folded back naturally, visible sewn fabric label, three-quarter top-down angle, rug pattern underneath, soft directional light, realistic fold shadow, tactile woven texture
```

**Best Catalogue Prompt Structure**

```text
[GLOBAL STYLE LOCK]

Scene:
[unique interior/background for this rug]

Product:
[specific rug design, colors, material, shape]

Shot template:
[one of the five shot instructions]

Quality control:
rug is the main subject, realistic scale, accurate woven textile texture, no warped geometry, no fake glossy surface, no extra patterns, no distorted furniture, no text unless specified
```

For the full set, keep the **global style lock** and **shot templates** fixed. Only change the **room/interior**, **rug design**, and small styling props.
